Kalamazoo Mennonite Missions

Kalamazoo Mennonite Fellowship seeks to extend ans support the work of God in the world in a number of ways. This is a list of missions, missionaries and service agencies we have gathered from our members that we especially want to lift up in prayer.

Local groups

  • Kalamazoo Loaves and Fishes. The mission of Kalamazoo Loaves and Fishes is "to expand the availability of emergency food resources and promte the ability of hungry people to feed themselves."
  • Campus Ministries at Western Michigan University: The River on Campus, Campus Crusade for Christ, His House, International Student Ministry, InterVarsity Christian Fellowship, Place of Praise.
  • Campus Ministries at Kalamazoo College, especially the Christian Students Fellowship.

Friends far and wide

  • Andy and Karen Weaver and family, serving with Wycliffe Bible Translators in Papua New Guinea.
  • David and Hilary Ker and family, serving with Wycliffe Bible Translators in Mozambique, Africa.
  • Joe and Sharon Rosa - principal and teacher/counselor in Guatamala.
  • JC and Lois Ebersole and family (Andrea and Kyle) with Eastern Mennonite Missions in Ghana working at Bible School and orphanage.
  • Josh Ewert with Mennonite Central Committee in Tanzania working with agriculture and water supply.
  • Gallern and Getrude: Gallern is a Zambian pastor and evangelist. He has planted several churches, works with the Zambian soap company, helps train pastors and heals the sick, gives to the poor, clothes the naked.
  • Carlos and Rebecca Pinto, Latin American Missions. Carlos and Rebecca serve in counseling and developing leaders with HCJB/World Radio Missionary Fellowship in Quito, Ecuador.
  • Gospel for Asia organizes missionaries who work in Asia among the most unreached people in the world. Pray for their outreaches, churches, Muslim workers, Christian schools, persecution, severe persecution in many places, funding, seminaries, Dalit work, relief work.
  • International Justice Mission is a human rights agency that secures justice for victims of slavery, sexual exploitation and other forms of violent oppression. IJM lawyers, investigators and aftercare professionals work with local officials to ensure immediate victim rescue and aftercare, to prosecute perpetrators and to promote functioning public justice systems.
  • Fr. John Halligan and the Working Boys Center in Quito, Ecuador.
  • Michael Ayuen Kuany and Rebuild Sudan. Michael is currently a student at Eastern Mennonite. The Rebuild Sudan organization plans to build a school in Jalle, Sudan.
